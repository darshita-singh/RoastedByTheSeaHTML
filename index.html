Where the fog meets the roast

Every morning the coastal mist rolls in before the burners do. We pull our greens from a handful of farms we've visited in person, then roast in batches small enough to taste every step.

Drop by the roastery on weekends. The cupping table is open, the kettle is on, and there's usually a dog asleep by the window.
